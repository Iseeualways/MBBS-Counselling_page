All India MBBS Counselling 2026

Conducted by the Medical Counselling Committee

The Medical Counselling Committee (MCC), under the Directorate General of Health Services (DGHS), Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, conducts the All India MBBS Counselling for candidates who qualify NEET UG 2026.

MCC conducts counselling for admission to:

  • 15% All India Quota (AIQ) MBBS seats in Government Medical Colleges
  • 100% MBBS seats in Deemed Universities
  • Central Universities, AIIMS & JIPMER
  • ESIC Medical Colleges (where applicable)
  • AFMS Medical Colleges (registration through MCC with separate admission process)
Note: Admission to 85% State Quota MBBS seats in Government Medical Colleges, and most Private Medical Colleges, is conducted by the respective State Counselling Authorities.
The All India MBBS counselling process is conducted in the following rounds:

The Stray Vacancy Round is conducted to fill MBBS seats that remain vacant after Round 3. This round is conducted online by MCC for participating institutions. Seat allotment is carried out according to NEET UG merit, seat availability, reservation policies where applicable, and MCC counselling regulations. Candidates allotted a seat during this round are required to complete the admission process within the prescribed timeline set by MCC.

Step By Step

All India NEET UG MBBS Counselling 2026 Process

The Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) conducts the All India NEET UG MBBS Counselling through a structured admission process. Although the counselling schedule and dates differ for each round, the overall procedure remains largely the same. Before every counselling round, MCC publishes the counselling schedule, seat matrix, and official notifications on its portal.

1

Online Registration

Candidates who wish to participate in the All India MBBS Counselling must complete the online registration within the specified dates for the respective counselling round. Fresh registration may be required for eligible counselling rounds as per the guidelines issued by MCC for the academic year 2026.

2

Payment of Counselling Fees

After successful registration, candidates must pay the applicable counselling fee through the online payment gateway.

    The counselling fee consists of:
  • Non-refundable Registration Fee
  • Refundable Security Deposit

Note: Candidates opting for both All India Quota and Deemed University counselling must pay the applicable fee for Deemed University participation.
3

Choice Filling

Registered candidates can select their preferred medical colleges and arrange them in the order of priority. During the choice filling period, candidates can add, remove, modify, or reorder their preferences until the deadline. Choice filling is conducted separately for every counselling round, and fresh choices must be submitted unless otherwise notified by MCC.

4

Choice Locking

After finalizing the list of preferred colleges, candidates must lock their choices before the specified deadline. Once the choices are locked, they cannot be edited for that counselling round. If candidates do not manually lock their choices, MCC may automatically lock the last saved preferences after the choice locking window closes.

5

Seat Allotment

Seat allotment is carried out based on several factors, including:

  • NEET UG 2026 rank
  • Candidate category
  • Reservation policy
  • Seat availability
  • Order of college Preferences

Candidates who are not allotted a seat in one round may participate in subsequent counselling rounds, subject to the eligibility criteria prescribed by MCC.

6

Seat Allotment Result

Once the allotment process is completed, MCC publishes the seat allotment result on its official website. Candidates who receive a seat can download their allotment letter and proceed with the admission process.

Note: MCC may release a provisional seat allotment result before publishing the final allotment list whenever required.
7

Reporting to the Allotted College

Candidates allotted an MBBS seat must report to the respective medical college within the prescribed reporting period. Failure to report within the stipulated deadline may result in cancellation of the allotted seat as per the counselling regulations.

    The admission process at the allotted college generally includes:
  • Document Verification
  • Submission of Original Documents
  • Payment of Admission Fees
  • Completion of College Admission Formalities

Karnataka MBBS Counselling 2026 Process

Conducted by the Karnataka Examinations Authority

The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) conducts the Karnataka MBBS Counselling for admission to medical colleges across the state. Candidates must qualify NEET UG 2026 and complete the KEA counselling registration to participate in the admission process. KEA conducts counselling for admission to Government Quota, Private Quota, Management Quota, and NRI Quota MBBS seats offered by Government and Private Medical Colleges in Karnataka.

To participate in the counselling process, candidates must register through the official KEA counselling portal, complete document verification, and fulfil all prescribed admission requirements within the notified schedule.
The Karnataka MBBS Counselling process is expected to be conducted in the following rounds:

KEA NEET UG MBBS Counselling 2026 Stages

The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) conducts the Karnataka MBBS Counselling through a structured admission process. Candidates are required to complete each stage within the prescribed timeline to remain eligible for seat allotment.

1

Online Registration

The counselling process begins with online registration through the official KEA portal. Registration is considered successful only after the application is submitted and the prescribed counselling fee is paid.

    During registration, candidates must:
  • Complete the online application form
  • Enter personal, academic and NEET UG details
  • Upload required documents, wherever applicable
  • Pay the counselling fee within the notified deadline
2

Document Verification

After registration, candidates must complete the document verification process according to the schedule announced by KEA. During this stage, the authority verifies the candidate's eligibility, reservation claims, and supporting documents. Candidates who successfully complete verification become eligible to participate in the subsequent stages of counselling.

3

Option Entry

Verified candidates can proceed with option entry, where they select and prioritize their preferred medical colleges. The order in which options are entered plays a significant role in the seat allotment process.

    During this stage, candidates can:
  • Select their preferred MBBS colleges
  • Arrange colleges in order of preference
  • Add, modify, or delete options until the option entry window closes.
4

Mock Seat Allotment

Before publishing the final allotment, KEA generally releases a mock seat allotment based on the options entered by candidates.

    The mock allotment helps candidates:
  • Estimate their probable seat allotment.
  • Evaluate their admission prospects.
  • Revise and optimize their college preferences before the final allotment.

The mock allotment is only indicative and does not guarantee the final seat allotment.

5

Final Seat Allotment

After the option entry period concludes, KEA publishes the final seat allotment results. Candidates can access and download their allotment letter through the official KEA portal.

    Seat allotment is determined based on:
  • NEET UG 2026 rank
  • Candidate Category
  • Reservation Policy
  • Availability of Seats
  • Order of College Preferences
6

Admission Confirmation

Candidates who are allotted an MBBS seat must complete all admission formalities within the prescribed reporting period. Failure to do so may result in cancellation of the allotted seat as per KEA guidelines.

    The admission process generally includes:
  • Exercising the appropriate admission option notified by KEA.
  • Payment of the prescribed admission fees.
  • Reporting to the allotted medical college.
  • Submission of original documents and completion of admission formalities.
Note : KEA follows a merit based seat allotment process that considers NEET UG rank, reservation policies, category wise seat availability, and the candidate's order of preferences. The complete allotment methodology is published in the official UGNEET Information Bulletin for the respective admission year.

MBBS Counselling Documents Required for 2026

Candidates participating in the NEET UG MBBS Counselling 2026 should keep the following original documents along with multiple sets of self attested photocopies ready for document verification and admission.

  • Class 10 Marks Card or Certificate (Proof of Date of Birth)
  • Class 12 Marks Card or Equivalent Qualifying Examination Certificate
  • NEET UG 2026 Admit Card
  • NEET UG 2026 Scorecard
  • NEET UG 2026 Rank Letter (If Issued)
  • NEET UG 2026 Application Form Confirmation Page
  • Provisional Seat Allotment Letter
  • Valid Government Issued Photo Identity Proof (Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving Licence)
  • Recent Passport Size Photographs
  • Transfer Certificate (TC)
  • Character Certificate
  • Migration Certificate (If Applicable)
  • Medical Fitness Certificate (If Required by the Allotted Institution)
  • Domicile Certificate (If Applicable)
  • Caste Certificate (If Applicable)
  • Income Certificate (If Applicable)
  • Economically Weaker Section (EWS) Certificate (If Applicable)
  • Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD) Certificate (If Applicable)
  • Non Creamy Layer (NCL) Certificate for OBC Candidates (If Applicable)

Note : The documents required for admission may vary depending on the counselling authority, such as the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) or the respective State Counselling Authority, the allotted medical college, and the candidate's reservation category. Candidates should carefully review the latest official counselling notification issued by the concerned authority before reporting for admission.
