The Process

Two authorities. One path to your seat.

Every MBBS aspirant in India moves through two possible counselling tracks — the national process run by the MCC, and the state process run by Karnataka's KEA. Here's how each one works.

MBBS All India Counselling

Conducted by the Medical Counselling Committee

  • Open only to students who have cleared the NEET‑UG entrance exam.
  • Fills All India Quota seats in every medical college, deemed and central universities, plus seats at AIIMS, JIPMER and other government institutes.
  • Students may be allotted any college across India, based on the priority order set during choice filling.
A Stray Vacancy Round fills whatever seats remain once the first three rounds are complete.

Karnataka MBBS Counselling

Conducted by the Karnataka Examinations Authority

  • Begins once NEET‑UG results are declared, and covers every government and private medical college seat in Karnataka.
  • Fills government quota, private quota, management quota and NRI quota seats.
  • Registration on KEA's official website is mandatory before Round 1 and Round 2, which run consecutively.
  • A Mop‑Up Round and Stray Vacancy Round follow if seats remain after the first two.
KEA publishes Mock Allotment Results ahead of the final Round 1 result — a preview of the college you're likely to be allotted, based on your choice list.
